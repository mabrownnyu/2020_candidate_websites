Increase capital and demand for America’s small businesses

Beto O’Rourke laid out critical measures to spur small business growth across the country and increase capital investment in businesses owned by women and people of color

Small businesses are the backbone of America’s economy. Ninety-nine percent of the nation’s businesses are small businesses, and the fastest-growing businesses over the past two decades are businesses run by women and people of color. Small and medium-size enterprises generate two-thirds of the country’s net new jobs, yet still face challenges in accessing critical capital and resources to drive their local economies – particularly in rural and underserved communities.

Beto’s career in public service has focused on the need to take on an economy that works too well for too few and fails to work for too many. From his start in El Paso as a small business owner himself to his work in Congress supporting increased access to private investment for America’s small businesses, Beto has put an unwavering pursuit of economic democracy and opportunity at the center of his career.

Inspired by the conversations he has had with small business owners around the country and drawing from his own experience, today O’Rourke laid out three specific actions he would take as part of his broader economic opportunity agenda to drive capital and demand for America’s small business with a particular focus on increasing investment for women and minority-owned businesses.

Increase Demand for Small Business Products and Services

Grow annual federal procurement from small businesses by $50B — and ensure at least half of that increase goes to businesses owned by women.

  • Currently, small business procurement is roughly $105 billion annually. Beto would increase this to $155 billion, and he would use his executive authority to increase the share of these contracts awarded to women-owned small businesses.  Today, that number is about $21 billion in federal contracts annually; Beto would increase this to $46 billion.

Increase Access to Capital for Women and Minority Owned Businesses to Start and Grow

Double the reach of the Community Development Financial Institutions Fund (CDFIs), which is a proven mechanism to increase access to capital in the places that need it most, by growing federal grants by 50 percent to $300 million, challenging the private sector to invest an equal amount into CDFIs, and focusing the new resources on women and minority entrepreneurs in particular.

  • Last year, the CDFI Fund received $200 million. Beto would propose an increase in annual funding to $300 million and do it in a way that catalyzes private investment into CDFIs — doubling their reach and impact and bringing total investment to $400 million.

Simplify Tax Rules and Requirements for All Small Businesses

Create a new standard deduction for small business owners allowing them to write off $50,000 in expenses—saving them money and time they can invest in growing and hiring.

  • Beto would work to enact this tax simplification, while reforming other aspects of the tax code to make it both fairer and more supportive of small businesses and small business owners, rather than the wealthy and well-connected.  In addition, Beto would establish a separate standard business expense deduction for independent workers and boost the support that start-ups receive through the tax code.

Outline

  • Increase Demand for Small Business Products and Services
  • Increase Access to Capital for Women and Minority Owned Businesses to Start and Grow
  • Simplify Tax Rules and Requirements for All Small Businesses
