Need to know

Who is Kirsten Gillibrand?

Kirsten Gillibrand is a U.S. senator from New York and has served in Congress for over 12 years. During that time, she’s proven she’s brave enough to take on tough fights no one else will—whether that’s repealing Don’t Ask, Don’t Tell, combating sexual assault, securing health benefits for 9/11 first responders, or voting against the Trump agenda more than any other senator or 2020 candidate. Kirsten will stand up to anyone to root out the corruption and greed that prevents progress in Washington, and she will never hesitate to fight for what’s right—even if it means she has to go it alone.

Kirsten is running to fix what’s broken in our country, and to fight for every family like it’s her own. She is the only candidate with both the bold vision and the experience to bring people together and enact the change we need.

Kirsten’s priorities

Health care: Kirsten believes that health care is a right, not a privilege, and has advocated for Medicare for all since 2005. As president, she’ll fight to get costs down and ensure everyone can access the health care they need.

Reproductive rights: Kirsten believes reproductive rights are nonnegotiable, and has led the 2020 field in defending them from Republicans’ attacks. She has pledged to only nominate judges who will commit to upholding Roe v. Wade, and was the first candidate to put out a reproductive rights agenda.

Climate change: Kirsten was an early champion of the Green New Deal, and as president she will get us to net-zero carbon emissions, stand up to fossil fuel special interests to protect our planet, and put a price on carbon.

LGBTQ rights: Kirsten has stood with the LGBTQ community throughout her career, and released the most comprehensive LGBTQ rights agenda of any 2020 candidate. As president, she’ll fight for the rights of LGBTQ people to live, work, adopt, raise families, serve in the military, and go to school free from discrimination or violence.

Gun violence prevention: Kirsten has fought hard to pass commonsense gun safety policies—including universal background checks, an assault weapons ban, and curbing gun trafficking—and to end the NRA’s chokehold on our laws.

Clean elections: Kirsten’s Clean Elections Plan is the critical structural change needed that will get big money and special interests out of politics, and ensure that elected officials are beholden only to voters.

The economy: Kirsten’s Family Bill of Rights is her economic plan to lift the financial burden on families and level the playing field for children. It includes national paid family leave, universal pre-K and affordable child care, and support for new parents.

Education: Kirsten’s national public service program will expand access to affordable college by rewarding students with two years of free tuition for every one year of public service.
